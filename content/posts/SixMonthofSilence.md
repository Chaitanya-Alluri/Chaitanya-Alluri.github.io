## 📚 The Reading Corner

Books became my anchor amidst the chaos — two very different reads, both worth talking about.

### 48 Laws of Power — Robert Greene
Picked up the **physical copy** (felt right for a book like this) and I'm about halfway through the abridged version. Many laws feel familiar in hindsight, but reading them laid out so plainly hits differently. It's not a comfortable book — but it's absolutely a worthwhile one.

### Elon Musk — Walter Isaacson
A **big read**, and I've been near the end for a while now — deliberately stretching it out. What gets me isn't the rockets or the companies. It's the *rhythm* of how he operates — staying decisive while juggling Tesla, SpaceX, X, and everything else simultaneously. Isaacson doesn't hold back, and the result is a raw, fascinating portrait. Highly recommended.

## 🎾 On the Court — A Season of Firsts

Tennis this season pushed me into new territory.

**Four tournaments. Four unforgettable experiences.**

- **GVK AISTA & Hyderabad Open ** — Both ended at the **quarterfinals**. Coudn't recall much :(

- **AISTA Bhimavaram — March 2026** *(First out-of-station tournament)*
  Traveling to compete in hometown, on unfamiliar courts, against unknown opponents — that's a different kind of commitment. Result: **Singles — Quarterfinals, Doubles — Semifinals.** Not a trophy, but a real tiring and great tournament of experience.

- **Vasanthanagar Club — Internal Doubles 🏆**
  Won this one Jan26-2026. After the quarterfinal exits, the universe balanced the ledger with a bit of luck. Winning among people who've trained alongside you, seen your bad days and your good ones — this one meant a lot.
